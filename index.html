Get Started

  • Utah's rock climbing spans seven key regions:

    • South Central: Indian Creek's famed cracks and San Rafael Swell's desert cliffs.
    • Southeast: Moab's sandstone towers like Castleton Tower.
    • Southwest: Zion's dramatic walls including Angels Landing.
    • West Desert: Remote spots like Ibex and Notch Peak.
    • Central: Maple Canyon's conglomerate rock and Tushar Mountains.
    • Northeast: Uinta Mountains' granite crags and alpine routes.
    • Wasatch Range: Convenient access to granite and limestone in areas like Little Cottonwood Canyon and American Fork Canyon.

    • Moab & Arches

    Moab and Arches National Park stand out as prime destinations for climbers in Utah. In Moab, adventurers tackle iconic formations like Castleton Tower and Fisher Towers, where crack climbing reigns supreme against a backdrop of stunning desert vistas. Meanwhile, Arches National Park offers a unique setting with its famed sandstone arches, providing climbers with exhilarating routes such as those around Delicate Arch. Amidst Utah's red rock landscapes, these areas offer not just thrilling climbs but also an unforgettable immersion into the rugged beauty of the desert terrain.

    American Fork Canyon

    American Fork Canyon, nestled in Utah's Wasatch Range, beckons climbers with its impressive limestone cliffs and convenient proximity to Salt Lake City. This area boasts a diverse array of routes, from challenging sport climbs to traditional routes, catering to climbers of all levels. Popular crags like Hellgate and The Dead Snag provide exhilarating experiences amidst stunning alpine scenery. With its accessibility and varied climbing options, American Fork Canyon offers a memorable adventure for climbers seeking both challenge and natural beauty in Utah's outdoor playground.

    Joe's Valley

    Joe's Valley, located in central Utah, is a premier destination for bouldering enthusiasts. Renowned for its top-tier sandstone problems, the area offers a diverse range of climbs suitable for climbers of all skill levels. Classic routes like "Low Tide" and "King Cobra" attract climbers from around the world, while the tranquil natural surroundings provide a serene backdrop for outdoor adventure. With its high-quality rock and breathtaking scenery, Joes Valley stands as an iconic bouldering destination, promising unforgettable experiences amidst Utah's rugged beauty.
